Our privacy promise, in plain language

We only ever see a site's name, never your history, never the page. The look-alike protection runs entirely in your browser and sends nothing anywhere. If you later sign in, the one thing that can leave the browser is a hostname, sent to exactly one endpoint to answer the live safety check, and it is not retained to build a browsing profile.

Your browser may say Whisper Guard can "read your browsing activity". That permission is how we see the site name to check it. We never read the page, never see what you type, and never store your history. There is no telemetry and no analytics in this extension.

What it catches, honestly

Already active: warnings before you type a password into a look-alike of a major brand's login page: homoglyph tricks, TLD swaps, hyphenation squats, and brand-impersonating subdomains, across 800+ heavily phished brands. Right-click any link to vet it before you open it.

Not magic: the on-device tier does not catch compromised legitimate sites or brand-new domains it has never heard of. The Whisper security graph covers far more. Sign in free to light up a live threat signal on every site you visit; it still says UNKNOWN for most of the web, because that is the truth.

You can revisit all of this any time in the extension's settings.
